The Stories

Institutional — Breaking
Goldman Sachs Launches Tokenized Real Estate Fund via GS DAP, Apex Group, and Archax

Goldman Sachs has partnered with Apex Group and UK-based digital asset exchange Archax to launch a tokenized real estate fund on Goldman's Digital Asset Platform (GS DAP). The fund is one of the most high-profile tokenized real estate products launched by a bulge-bracket bank to date, reflecting Goldman's accelerating move from blockchain infrastructure builder to active product issuer. Archax, which holds FCA approval as a regulated digital securities exchange, provides the trading and custody infrastructure. This is the institutional real estate tokenization use case that the sector has been waiting for a top-five investment bank to execute. Infrastructure · via RWA.xyz
Japan Completes First Real-Time DVP Settlement for Security Tokens Using DCJPY

Japan has completed its first real-time Delivery vs. Payment (DVP) settlement for tokenized securities using DCJPY, a digital yen developed by a consortium of Japanese banks. DVP settlement — where the security and the payment transfer simultaneously, eliminating settlement risk — is one of the core technical benefits of tokenized securities over traditional T+2 settlement. Japan completing this on a digital currency rail is the clearest demonstration yet that the infrastructure for atomic settlement is live and functioning outside the US. Source: RWA.xyz News → DTCC July 2026 coverage

Breaking · Recap
SpaceX SPCX: One Week On — What We Learned

Last week's SpaceX IPO launched simultaneously on Nasdaq and multiple blockchains in the most-watched tokenized equity event in history. One week on: the Backpack/Sunrise Solana version (1:1 backed by real shares, full ACATS redemption) has emerged as the structurally soundest product. The xStocks versions from Bybit and Kraken have confirmed their unavailability to US, Canadian, UK, and Australian residents. OKX and Coinbase perpetual futures are price exposure only — not equity. The market has begun pricing the products differently based on their actual legal structures, which is exactly how a maturing tokenized equities market should function. → Full SpaceX analysis

Curve Finance · via RWA.xyz
Curve Founder Proposes On-Chain Debt Tokenization Mechanism

Curve Finance founder Michael Egorov has proposed a debt tokenization mechanism built on Curve's infrastructure, adding the DeFi-native lending angle to the debt tokenization category. The proposal would allow structured debt to be tokenized and used as collateral within DeFi protocols — extending the RWA/DeFi composability that protocols like Centrifuge and Maple Finance have been building toward. The Curve founder's involvement signals that DeFi's most sophisticated builders are increasingly treating RWA as a primary use case rather than a niche vertical. Source: RWA.xyz News

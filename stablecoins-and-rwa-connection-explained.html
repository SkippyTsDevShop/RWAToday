Stablecoins and RWA Are the Same Infrastructure. Here Is How They Connect.

Stablecoins are the plumbing. RWA is what flows through it. Understanding how USDC, USDY, USDS, and bank-issued stablecoins connect to tokenized real-world assets is fundamental to understanding how the sector actually works.

📅 2026-06-10⏱ 7 min read🏷 Analysis · Stablecoins · RWA

Stablecoins and real-world asset tokenization are not separate categories. They are the same infrastructure viewed from two different angles. Understanding how they connect is one of the most important things an RWA investor or builder can understand about how the sector actually works.

The simple version: stablecoins are the plumbing. RWA is what flows through it.

What Stablecoins Actually Are in This Context

A stablecoin is a token designed to maintain a fixed value — almost always $1. USDC and USDT are the most widely used. Combined, they represent over $200 billion in circulating supply. They are how dollar-denominated value moves on blockchains: when you buy a tokenized Treasury, you pay in stablecoins. When yield is distributed to token holders, it's distributed in stablecoins. When you want to exit a DeFi position, you receive stablecoins.

In traditional finance, every transaction goes through a settlement layer — a bank, a custodian, a clearinghouse. In on-chain finance, stablecoins are that settlement layer. They are the medium through which everything else is priced, transferred, and settled.

The Stablecoin Ecosystem — Major Players in 2026
  • USDC (Circle) — regulated, dollar-backed, primary institutional settlement coin. $45B+ circulation.
  • USDT (Tether) — largest by volume, primarily used in trading and international transfers. $110B+ circulation.
  • USDY (Ondo Finance) — yield-bearing stablecoin backed by Treasuries. The first stablecoin that pays you to hold it.
  • USDS / sUSDS (Sky/MakerDAO) — DeFi stablecoin backed partly by tokenized RWAs. The first major stablecoin with RWA collateral.
  • JLTXX (JPMorgan) — institutional tokenized money market fund designed for stablecoin reserve use.

How RWA Depends on Stablecoins

Every tokenized RWA transaction touches stablecoins at some point. When an investor buys OUSG from Ondo Finance, they send USDC. When OUSG distributes yield, it comes back as USDC. When the position is redeemed, it settles in USDC. The RWA is the asset. The stablecoin is the currency.

This dependency runs deeper than just transaction settlement. Stablecoins are also used as:

Pricing references — the value of a tokenized bond is expressed in dollar-equivalent stablecoins. Oracle prices that feed RWA protocols are denominated in stablecoins.

Liquidity pools — on decentralized exchanges, tokenized assets pair with stablecoins to create tradeable markets. PAXG/USDC pools on Uniswap are how tokenized gold gets its on-chain price discovery.

Collateral — stablecoins are accepted as collateral in lending protocols. RWA tokens are increasingly being accepted alongside them. The two assets sit in the same collateral pools.

How Stablecoins Depend on RWA

This is the part of the relationship that's less obvious and more important.

Traditional stablecoins — USDC and USDT — are backed by dollar-denominated assets: bank deposits, Treasury bills, short-term commercial paper. The companies that issue them hold billions in Treasury bills as their reserves. Those Treasury bills are, structurally, real-world assets backing the stablecoin. The stablecoin's stability depends entirely on the quality and liquidity of those RWA reserves.

MakerDAO took this further deliberately. Its USDS stablecoin includes tokenized Treasury products (including Ondo's OUSG) as part of its collateral base. This means the DeFi ecosystem's most widely used collateral-backed stablecoin is partly backed by tokenized RWA products. The on-chain economy and the real-world economy are already intertwined at the reserve level.

"Every USDC in circulation is backed by a Treasury bill in a bank account. Every Treasury bill in a bank account is a real-world asset. Stablecoins have always been RWA — most people just haven't framed it that way."

The GENIUS Act: Why Regulation of Stablecoins Is Regulation of RWA Infrastructure

The GENIUS Act, which passed the Senate Banking Committee in May 2026, establishes a federal licensing framework for payment stablecoins in the United States. Most coverage framed this as stablecoin regulation. It is equally significant for the RWA sector.

Here's why: the GENIUS Act will enable US banks to issue licensed stablecoins backed by their balance sheets. When JPMorgan, Bank of America, or Citibank issues a bank-backed stablecoin, it will be used to settle tokenized security transactions on platforms like the DTCC's forthcoming infrastructure. The settlement rail for RWA transactions will become bank-issued, federally regulated, and institutionally credible.

The Yield-Bearing Stablecoin: Where the Categories Merge Completely

USDY is where the distinction between "stablecoin" and "RWA product" completely dissolves. USDY is:

— A stablecoin (it's worth approximately $1 and is used as a dollar substitute)
— An RWA product (it's backed by US Treasury bills, which are real-world assets)
— A yield product (it accrues ~4–5% annually, distributed daily)
— A DeFi primitive (it can be used as collateral and in liquidity pools)

This convergence is where the sector is heading. The long-term trajectory is a world where most stablecoins are yield-bearing, backed by high-quality RWA collateral, and fully composable in DeFi. USDY is the prototype. JLTXX is the institutional version. USDS with RWA collateral is the DeFi-native version.

The traditional image of "stablecoin" as a parking spot for idle cash is being replaced by yield-bearing, RWA-backed digital dollars that generate returns while maintaining price stability. That's a fundamentally different financial primitive — and it's already live.

What This Means for RWA Investors

Practical Implications
  • Evaluate stablecoin reserves: Before using any stablecoin for RWA settlement, check what backs it. USDC and USDT publish reserve attestations. Understand the quality of the underlying assets.
  • Consider yield-bearing stablecoins: If you hold USDC between investments, you're earning 0%. USDY holders earn ~4–5% on the same dollar-equivalent value. The idle cash argument for non-yield-bearing stablecoins is weakening.
  • Watch bank stablecoin announcements: The first US bank to launch a licensed stablecoin will set the template for institutional RWA settlement. This will likely happen in H2 2026.
  • Understand USDS/sUSDS: If you use MakerDAO's ecosystem, you're already exposed to RWA collateral. Know which RWA assets back the stablecoin you're holding.

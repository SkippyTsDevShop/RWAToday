Element United: Tokenizing the Earth's Resources — From Mine to Market, On-Chain

Most of the world's natural resources — gold, copper, jade, carbon credits — move through supply chains with almost no verifiable transparency. Scott Lomu co-founded Element United to change that. The vision: every certified mine, every offset, every natural resource asset leaving a verifiable on-chain trail from source to market.

RW
RWAToday Staff
RWAToday.news Editorial Team
· May 20, 2026 · 8 min read

The global mining industry has a transparency problem. Gold mined in conflict zones enters supply chains that serve luxury jewelry markets. Carbon offsets are sold, retired, and in some cases sold again. Rare earth minerals with significant environmental extraction costs reach consumer electronics with no traceable record of how they were sourced. The gap between what happens at the mine and what gets reported to the market is not a rounding error — it is the defining accountability failure of the global resource economy.

The resources Element United is building infrastructure for aren't abstract commodities on a trading screen. They are in your pocket right now. Cobalt — mined primarily in the Democratic Republic of Congo — is in the lithium-ion battery in every smartphone and every electric vehicle on the road. Copper runs through the wiring of every building you enter. Rare earth elements power the screen you're reading this on. We are all participants in the natural resource supply chain, as consumers, whether we know it or not. The question is whether the chain that connects mine to product is one we can trust — and today, for the vast majority of resources, it is not.

Scott Lomu spent fourteen years working in the gold mining industry — in West Africa, North America, and South America — before he became convinced that blockchain technology offered a structural solution to this accountability problem. In 2022, he co-founded Element United with a mission that is both ambitious and precise: to decarbonize and rehabilitate the planet by building verified, on-chain markets for natural resources and carbon offsets. You can follow his work on LinkedIn.

"Green tech innovation spurs economic growth and draws vital international investments for climate resilience," Lomu has said, "but effective carbon trading hinges on informed partners and stakeholders." Element United is building the infrastructure to make those stakeholders informed at a level that has never previously been achievable — source-level, blockchain-verified, continuously auditable.

Scott Lomu — The Builder Behind the Mission

Scott Lomu's background is unusual in the blockchain space. Most founders of RWA tokenization companies come from finance, law, or technology. Lomu came from the mine floor — spending over a decade working in gold mining operations across three continents before co-founding Element United. That operational background shapes the company's approach in ways that matter.

Element United is not building theoretical infrastructure for a problem it has read about. It is building practical solutions for the accountability gaps Lomu observed firsthand during fourteen years in an industry where the distance between extraction and end market is precisely where trust failures occur. Recognized on the Silicon Slope as one of green tech's newest thought leaders, Lomu has assembled a team and advisory board that bridges the frontier world of resource extraction with next-generation blockchain solutions.

The Element United Product Suite

Element United has built four interconnected products that together form an on-chain resource verification ecosystem.

Offset™ is a carbon marketplace built on proof. Where most carbon credit markets have struggled with verification — credits being sold, retired, and in some cases double-counted — Offset tracks and verifies carbon credits through source data, ensuring what is sold is real and what is retired stays retired. The platform uses blockchain's immutability to create a permanent, auditable record of every credit issued, traded, and retired.

Core™ is the tokenization engine for utility. It delivers digitally native, blockchain-verified access to resource-linked assets — metals, minerals, and raw materials — without compromising integrity or compliance. Core is designed for utility, not speculation: the goal is to create verifiable digital representations of physical resources that give buyers confidence in what they are actually purchasing.

Alchemy™ is a minting engine for luxury, legacy, and proof. It turns certified resources into immutable digital assets — minted on-chain, authenticated at the source. The target market for Alchemy includes luxury goods, fine jewelry, and provenance-sensitive markets where authentication is a premium feature.

Element Solutions is the integrated product platform that brings the three tools together, providing businesses and mining partners with the full stack of verification, tokenization, and marketplace access.

The ELMT Token and the Element Blockchain

ELMT is a true utility token, governed by the Element Node Network's Distributed Governance Framework — a community-owned structure composed of Element Node Owners. This governance architecture aligns incentives between network participants and the broader mission of the platform.

Token distribution occurs on the Element Blockchain and is subject to the DGF Charter, which operates independently of Element United DAO LLC. Node owners earn ELMT digital rewards daily by running Element nodes — rewards that can be bridged into ELMT ERC-20 tokens. ELMT is also available on major exchanges and listed on CoinMarketCap and CoinGecko, with swap capability directly from the Element wallet.

The decentralized governance structure is not incidental to the product's value proposition. For a platform whose core purpose is verifying the integrity of physical resource supply chains, having a governance structure that cannot be controlled or manipulated by any single entity — including Element United itself — is the architecture of trust. The DGF Charter creates a system where the network's integrity is structurally guaranteed rather than dependent on corporate good faith.

Partner Mines and the On-Chain Resource Economy

Element United's most ambitious vision goes beyond building a platform — it is building a new economic model for resource extraction itself. The company's partner mine program brings certified mines into the Element ecosystem, allowing the physical assets in the ground to generate on-chain economic value without necessarily requiring that they be extracted.

"We believe that the blockchain economy we build on top of a mine can become more beneficial than actual mining," Lomu has said. "That's the beauty of it — the elements stay in the ground. It's a boon for everyone involved: digital miners, local communities, and of course, our environment."

This model has been demonstrated with Element United's first gold mining partner at the Manson Creek Mine, and with a 3,000-acre jade mine claim that committed its entire jade portfolio to Element United. The jade mine partnership illustrates the breadth of the model: it applies not just to metals and carbon but to any certified natural resource that benefits from verified provenance and on-chain accessibility.

Why Element United Belongs in the RWA Conversation

The RWA market in 2026 is dominated by discussion of tokenized treasuries, money market funds, and institutional financial instruments. These are important — and RWAToday covers them extensively. But the deeper thesis of real-world asset tokenization is that blockchain can bring trust, transparency, and liquidity to any physical asset that currently suffers from opacity and inefficiency.

Natural resources — gold, copper, jade, carbon credits, rare earths — are among the most important physical assets in the global economy, and among the most opaque. The distance from extraction to end market is precisely where accountability fails: conflict minerals enter clean supply chains, carbon credits get double-counted, premium certifications get misrepresented. These are not edge cases. They are systemic features of an industry that has never had the infrastructure to verify claims at scale.

Element United is building that infrastructure. Source-certified, blockchain-secured metals, minerals, and carbon offsets — tokenized and ready for global markets. The mission is clear, the technology is live, and the team has the operational credibility to execute it.

In an RWA market that is increasingly asking what the next frontier looks like beyond treasuries and real estate, natural resource tokenization — done with the verification rigor that Element United is building — is one of the most compelling answers.

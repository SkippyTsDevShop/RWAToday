Tokenized Gold vs Physical Gold vs Gold ETF: A Direct Comparison

PAXG, a gold bar, or GLD shares — three ways to own gold, three completely different risk profiles. Here is a direct comparison: counterparty risk, fees, liquidity, trading hours, and which is right for your situation.

📅 2026-06-10⏱ 7 min read🏷 Analysis · Commodities · RWA

You want gold exposure. You have three options in 2026: buy a physical gold bar, buy shares in a gold ETF like GLD or IAU, or buy a tokenized gold product like PAXG or XAUT. Each represents ownership of gold. Each works very differently. The right choice depends on what you're actually trying to accomplish.

This is a direct comparison — no hype, no agenda. Just what each option actually is, what it costs, how it settles, and what risks it carries.

Physical Gold: The Original Hard Asset

Physical gold is gold you can hold: coins, bars, bullion. You own it outright. No counterparty. No platform risk. No issuer who can go bankrupt and take your gold with them. When every other system fails, gold in your hand is still gold in your hand.

The costs are real. A 1-troy-ounce gold coin from a reputable dealer in 2026 costs approximately $3,350–3,400 — a 2–3% premium over spot price. Storage either costs money (a bank safe deposit box or a private vault runs $100–500/year) or creates security risk if stored at home. Insurance adds more. Selling requires finding a buyer, paying a dealer's spread, and waiting for payment. Shipping gold internationally is a logistical and customs exercise.

Physical Gold — Summary
  • What you own: The physical metal, directly
  • Counterparty risk: None — you hold it
  • Buy premium: 2–5% over spot
  • Storage cost: $100–500/year or self-storage risk
  • Liquidity: Low — hours to days to sell at fair price
  • Minimum investment: ~$100 (fractional coins) to $3,300+ (1 oz bar)
  • Trading hours: Business hours at dealers
  • Best for: Long-term store of value, zero counterparty risk, geographic portability

Gold ETFs: Market Access, Fund Risk

A gold ETF like SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) or iShares Gold Trust (IAU) holds physical gold in custodial vaults on behalf of shareholders. When you buy GLD shares, you own a proportional claim on that gold pool. The price tracks spot gold closely. You can buy and sell instantly during market hours through any brokerage account.

The costs are low but real. GLD charges a 0.40% annual management fee; IAU charges 0.25%. These are deducted from NAV continuously, meaning your gold exposure slowly decays relative to spot over long holding periods. The fee seems small but over a decade compounds to meaningful underperformance versus holding gold directly.

The key structural limitation: ETF gold trades only during market hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM EST, Monday–Friday). Gold markets themselves trade 24 hours globally. When a geopolitical event moves gold prices at 2 AM Sunday, ETF holders can't act until Monday morning. Tokenized gold holders can act immediately.

Gold ETF (GLD/IAU) — Summary
  • What you own: Shares in a fund that holds gold. Not the gold itself.
  • Counterparty risk: Custodian (HSBC for GLD, JPMorgan for IAU) and fund structure
  • Annual fee: 0.25–0.40% of holdings
  • Liquidity: High during market hours — instant sell at market price
  • Minimum investment: ~1 share (~$30 for IAU, ~$175 for GLD)
  • Trading hours: Market hours only — Mon–Fri 9:30–4:00 EST
  • Best for: Low-friction gold exposure in a traditional brokerage, tax-advantaged accounts

Tokenized Gold: 24/7 Settlement, DeFi Composability

Tokenized gold — primarily PAXG (Paxos Gold) and XAUT (Tether Gold) — represents one troy ounce of physically allocated gold held in regulated vaults, with each token redeemable for the underlying metal. The token lives on a blockchain (PAXG on Ethereum, XAUT on Ethereum and Tron), trades on crypto exchanges, and settles in minutes at any time of day or night.

The key innovations over ETFs: you can hold fractional tokens (0.001 PAXG = 0.001 troy oz of gold), trade 24/7 globally including weekends and holidays, transfer peer-to-peer without a broker, and — critically for RWA investors — use tokenized gold as collateral in DeFi lending protocols. A position in PAXG can generate yield by being deposited as collateral on Aave or other platforms, something a gold ETF position cannot do.

"PAXG holders can request physical delivery of the underlying gold bar. That option doesn't exist with a gold ETF. The token is closer to allocated gold ownership than a fund share ever was."
Tokenized Gold (PAXG/XAUT) — Summary
  • What you own: Specific allocated gold in a vault — PAXG is redeemable for physical delivery
  • Counterparty risk: Issuer (Paxos/Tether) and custodian vault — plus smart contract risk
  • Annual fee: 0.15% for PAXG (creation/redemption fee); no ongoing management fee
  • Liquidity: High — trades on major crypto exchanges 24/7
  • Minimum investment: Fractions of 1 oz — accessible from ~$33 (0.01 oz)
  • Trading hours: 24/7/365 — no market close, no holidays
  • DeFi composability: Yes — usable as collateral, in liquidity pools
  • Best for: 24/7 gold exposure, DeFi integration, international access, fractional ownership

The Direct Comparison

Physical vs ETF vs Tokenized — Head to Head
  • Counterparty risk: Physical = none. ETF = fund + custodian. Tokenized = issuer + custodian + smart contract
  • 24/7 trading: Physical = no. ETF = no. Tokenized = yes ✅
  • Fractional ownership below $100: Physical = limited. ETF = yes (IAU ~$30/share). Tokenized = yes ✅
  • DeFi composability: Physical = no. ETF = no. Tokenized = yes ✅
  • Physical redemption: Physical = you have it. ETF = no. PAXG = yes ✅
  • Tax treatment (US): Physical = collectibles rate (28%). ETF = capital gains. Tokenized = varies — consult a tax professional
  • Regulatory clarity: Physical = clear. ETF = clear. Tokenized = improving but jurisdiction-dependent

Which Is Right for You?

If zero counterparty risk is your priority: physical gold. Nothing beats holding the metal directly for pure counterparty-free exposure.

If you want frictionless gold in a traditional portfolio: gold ETF. Lowest friction, works in any brokerage, fits in an IRA or 401k.

If you want 24/7 trading, DeFi integration, or you're based outside the US: tokenized gold. PAXG and XAUT are globally accessible, settle instantly, and can generate additional yield through DeFi composability that ETFs structurally cannot.

All three can coexist in a portfolio. The choice is not ideological — it's functional. What are you trying to do with the gold position? Answer that question first, and the right format becomes clear.
